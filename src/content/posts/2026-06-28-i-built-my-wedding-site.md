Hey folks, 

Are you married? or in near future? or never? or whatever...

I am marrying soon. One morning, (you know where ideas flow) I envisioned building a website, which opens like red-curtain on the stage, and pops-up Shiva weds........ I was so much into this idea, and I started looking for other people, whether building your own wedding site is kind of stupid or a good idea. 

Lol, it wasn't only me, who is stupid. Looks like, there are other like-minded people like me. And, even more funnier is, I tell you this, there exists very niche-driven "build-your-own-wedding site" businesses. Wow, super cool....

I guess, those "out-of-the-box" wedding site builders are targetted for non-techie people who wants to have their wedding site. They come up with some pre-defined wedding things, like, Guests RSVP, etc etc etc. I read through some reddit folks on their experience, and the debate on "which" wedding-site builder/provider is "customizable" or "better". 

For me, It's not going to work, because, the way I have imagined, isn't something can be achievable through them. I need to do it myself; specially because, those wedding sites are more-or-less targetted to western world, and so, Nepali marriages are different, culturally, procedures, and everything. 

But then, I though, is there 'Nepali wedding site' building providers? (apart from some software companies).

I found [nepwed.com,](https://nepwed.com/) but it is more wedding planning platform:

> Nepal's premier wedding planning platform. Find the best vendors, manage
>  your guest list, and plan your dream Nepali wedding — all in one place.



Then, I remembered I wasn't utilizing my claude subscription to it's full-potential. Let's play with him. 

I started planning with some other experts (chatgpt, ...) explaining my vision, and convincing that it should be different from typical wedding-site, since we are not designing as if western marriages happens, rather than trying to reflect the Nepali Cultural wedding. 

Reason for me to go with my idea, was pretty much valuable; since I have *few international guests, who (hopefully) will attend my marriage. I want to give them the authentic Nepali wedding experience. And, also, since they are not used to to our cultural process, and everything, this will be their "truth-of-source" for navigating and understanding the process even before they are attending themselves. 

Just to get "confirmaiton bias", I shared my this vision to my french colleague during a lunchtime. He said, 'c'est incroyable!!!'

Then, I showed him the initial version on mobile (desktop-view is more elegant though), and his reaction was, "wow, I love this, I love the music, and I love this... "

I hope, I will be able to convince him to attend my marriage, with the help of my virtual-wedding site. I really want to mimic the true-cultural process, so that my guests get pretty much of 'what to expect'. Plus, hope this will help them prepare better.
